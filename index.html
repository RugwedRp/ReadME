What is a coder?


Coder is a professional who uses programming languages to communicate with computers and software and make them perform certain tasks.Computers use machine code to process information. coders write in programming languages that a computer can convert into machine language to follow a code's instructions.Coders may know just one or a few of these languages, which they use to build software. Computers, phones, modern cars, aeroplanes, security systems and devices use this software.

What is the difference between a coder and a programmer?

The terms coder and programmer are sometimes used interchangeably. However, they are different. Coding is a subset of programming. All programmers are coders, however not all coders are programmers. Programmers have a more in-depth understanding of different programming languages. They use these languages to develop and execute different functions or applications. Programmers also solve problems that arise from code. Programming usually involves more creativity and conceptualising than coding.


How to become a coder?

To understand how to become a coder online, you can choose various paths. Some candidates complete their undergraduate courses in relevant areas, while others sign up for online training or courses. If you are planning to become an online coder, use these steps:


1. Start your learning process

Those who are new to coding can seek free online resources and start gaining some fundamental knowledge of their own. Countless online coding resources provide valuable career advice and extra insights into coding and programming. These online resources can provide valuable coding lessons. You can practice exercises relating to different programming languages. This can help in fastening the learning process. Also, a free online resource can help you understand whether you can commit to it before you spend money on a training program or a degree.


2. Choose the right programming languages

Most employers expect candidates to have proficiency in JavaScript, HTML and CSS. So, focus on picking at least one or more to become proficient. Beyond that, the number of additional languages you pursue depends on the career path you want to follow. Learning and mastering over two languages let you switch between careers and enhances your career growth. Start by learning HTML and CSS because these two are the primary scripting languages. In the next step, start by creating an interactive website. For this, focus on learning Python, PHP and JavaScript.


3. Complete your education

After learning the basics of some fundamental languages, focus on completing your education. Many graduate-level courses like Bachelor of Science (BSc), Bachelor of Computer Application (BCA) and Bachelor of Engineering (BE) can help you gain mastery over different languages widely used by coders. Most of these courses take anywhere from three to four years to complete when you study them full time. This course covers more than coding and gives you a basic understanding of different programming languages.


4. Know your focus area

As you increase your knowledge about coding, it is essential to think about the coder or developer you want to become. Knowing how you want to apply your coding skills can help you choose a focus area of your interest. Generally, coders can choose from one of the three career tracks, including:

  • Front-end developers:

    • These professionals focus on the client-side development of a website. They primarily focus on designing and building the overall structure of a website.

  • Back-end developers:

    • These professionals focus on the back-end or server-side of websites and applications. The primary duty of these professionals is to ensure that the website functions as it's intended to.

  • Full-stack developers:

    • A full-stack developer is responsible for full-stack development. Usually, these professionals handle both front-end and back-end development processes.
